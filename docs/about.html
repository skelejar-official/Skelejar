About Skelejar

This project is dedicated to the study of narrative structure, character depth, and the literary concept of the tragic flaw as applied to modern antagonists.

The Tragic Flaw

In classical literature, a **tragic flaw** (or *hamartia*) is a personality trait—often pride or hubris—that leads to a hero's downfall. Skelejar inverts this concept, applying it to villains. We believe that no antagonist is truly invulnerable; their deepest weakness is often a magnified reflection of their greatest strength or most cherished belief.

A villain's flaw isn't always a moral one, but often an **intellectual blind spot**. For instance, an obsession with order might lead a villain to discount the unpredictability of human emotion (as seen with Thanos), or a need for an audience might prevent a villain from taking the final, necessary step toward true victory (as seen with The Joker).

How We Analyze

  • **Inversion:** We identify the villain's primary goal (e.g., control, chaos, peace) and look for the internal element that contradicts or undermines it.
  • **The Critical Moment:** We pinpoint the exact moment in the narrative where the villain's plan failed, tracing that failure back to a specific choice driven by the flaw.
  • **The Narrative Irony:** We highlight the irony that the villain's own character, rather than the hero's direct action, was the instrument of their defeat.
