Exposing the Single Flaw

Exposing the Single Flaw in Great Villains.

Palpatine (Darth Sidious)

Hubris

Star Wars Trilogy

The Flaw: The Arrogance of Corruption

Palpatine's singular flaw is his **overconfidence in his ability to corrupt and control others**. This Hubris leads him to engage in unnecessary theatrics (such as luring Luke Skywalker to the Death Star) and to openly gloat. By indulging his ego and the desire to witness the final corruption of the chosen one, he exposed himself to the very emotional connection (father-son) that ultimately led to his downfall.

Howard Payne

Pragmatism

Speed

The Flaw: Over-Engineering the Transaction

Howard Payne is purely pragmatic: his goal is the money. His flaw lies in his **over-reliance on complex mechanics and transaction monitoring**. Instead of simply taking the money and disappearing, he creates a convoluted, multi-stage scheme involving a bus, a bank transfer, and a subway car. This pragmatic need to control every single variable, including personally verifying the money transfer, is what ultimately exposes him and gives Jack Traven multiple opportunities to predict his next move.
